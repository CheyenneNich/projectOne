  1. 23, 000 in 24

    We've heard for years, if not decades, that we are killing the trees. While there are some large companies and organizations that are doing their part, it is nothing quite like 23 year old Antoine Moses.The young Quebecer broke world records when he planted 23, 060 trees in 24 hours. The previous record was 15, 170 set in 2001. Moses is a young althele, triaining for an upcoming Ironman race, and now a generational hero.

  2. Artwork saves lives

    A mural painted at a "daunting and dangerous" intersection has helped reduce the average speed by half. 65% of people that live in the community now say they feel very safe which is up from the 25% before the mural was painted. Bloomberg Philanthropies conducted the research. The organization also has an Asphalt Art Inititive that is transforming public spaces into art. With the right approach they could de-stigmatize street art.

  4. Credit given where credit is due

    Thanks to various POC social media influencers black women are finally being recognized for popularizing nail art. Originating in 1950, nail art became wildly popular because of famous black women throught history. Model Donyale Luna on the cover of a magazine in 1966, singers Diana Ross and Donna Summer in the 1970s, La Toya Jackson and Flo Jo in the 80's and 90's. The current new wave nail art was solidified by Missy Elliott and Lil Kim.
